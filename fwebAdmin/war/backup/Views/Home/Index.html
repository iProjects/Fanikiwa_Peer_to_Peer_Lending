How peer-to-peer lending work.

Also known as social lending or lend-to-save, peer-to-peer lending works by individual lenders and borrowers coming together to get better rates.
Fanikiwa's peer-to-peer loans offer borrowers competitive low rates and flexible terms.
Lenders earn higher returns by lending to borrowers.
Peer-to-peer lending sites match up savers, who are willing to lend, with borrowers - either individuals or small businesses.
Rates can be better than those offered by banks - as high as about 16% for savers, and as low as about 5% for borrowers on a five-year loan.
Peer-To-Peer Lending, which is a concept that has been around for a very long time, but the method as to how it was carried out was not always efficient at all. It went a little something like this:
Friend #1: Hey best friend, can I borrow 100,000 to get a new car?
Friend #2: Do you promise to pay me back?
Friend #1: Of course, you know that I am good for it!
Friend #2: Ok fine, I will get the money to you by next week.
The truth is that these situations are very common.
We enter into lending/borrowing agreements with friends and families all the time, and the lender enters into a situation with unlimited risk and no reward for their risk.
This is where a website like Fanikiwa comes into play.
Fanikiwa is a peer-to-peer lending network that brings investors and borrowers together to satisfy both parties needs by offering a secure, legal, and efficient service. Those people who wish to take more control of their investments can lend money to borrowers, and borrowers who are tired of having limited options for choosing a personal loan product can try Fanikiwa .
